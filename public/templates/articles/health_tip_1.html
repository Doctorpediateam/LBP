Back-Up Your Health with These Three Stretches

The human body does a tremendous job of keeping us going, even when we do not listen to it.

We walk to work, hike on nature trails, and run errands – all while trying to have good posture. We lift heavy objects correctly, get eight hours of sleep a night, and maintain a healthy weight, until one day something gets tweaked and suddenly there is that dreaded twinge in the lower back. We know something just happened. We may have even heard a ‘pop.’

What do we do? Most of us keep going, work through the pain, and push ourselves until one morning we are just unable to get up out of bed. The back muscles have rebelled. They want you to pay attention to them and your body.

We are not born with strong back muscles, and they do not just happen overnight.   Those who take the time to do daily gentle back stretches reap the rewards of a flexible, durable back and cut the risk of experiencing lower back pain.

Get rid of that heavy handbag, and take the time to add stretches to your day-to-day routine proactively.

It is okay to fall in love with taking care of yourself!

Here are some tender, kind, and peaceful ways to stretch out your back muscles. Remember the overall objective is to start slow and go with the flow, building on the stretches as a starting point. Your healthcare provider can advise you before beginning any new exercise program.

Child’s Pose is a simple, classic stretch that builds the posture of the spine.  Kneel down on a padded mat, knees bent, and buttocks sitting on your heels. Glide your arms along the floor out in front of you. Feel your back arc one vertebra at a time. Breathe in and out. Hold the pose for a few moments and stop if there is any discomfort.  

Cat Stretch is your chance to purr! Place your hands and knees on a padded mat and move the top of your head toward the floor then arch your back. This pose resembles a content cat stretching after a long nap. Repeat as often as comfortable. What a purrfect way to stretch out a number of your body’s muscles along the spine. Meow!

For the Wall Posture, all you need is a wall! Find a wall, fence, or office partition divider with enough free space for your body. Place your back flat up against the wall.

Place the heels of your feet a few inches away from the wall and separate your feet by about six inches. Get a good solid stance. Put your hands, palms down on the wall behind you, so they are touching the wall. Look straight ahead. Done! Hold the position, feeling your spine straighten and align. In your office, home, or on a city street, this stretch reminds you to maintain a good posture. It leaves an imprint of how to align your shoulder, arms, and back, so that you may better support your spine.

These are just a sampling of great ways to help you get used to stretching and building a bridge to the next layer: strengthening the muscles in your back.
