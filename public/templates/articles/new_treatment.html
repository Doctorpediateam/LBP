New Treatments and Therapies in Development for Lower Back Pain

Healthcare providers are instrumental in the treatment and care of pain management patients. New developments in individualized, targeted therapies in the field of biologic response modifiers are innovative, patient centric treatments. These include monoclonal antibodies, targeted mu-receptor agonists, stem cell analogs, and sono-sensitive systems.   

Tanezumab is a humanized monoclonal antibody, under development by Pfizer and Eli Lilly. Also referred to as MABs, Moabs or mAbs, the immunotherapy classification recognizes specialized proteins associated with the pain cascade. In this case, the protein is Nerve Growth Factor. Tanezumab selectively binds to NGF, blocking activation of pain impulses. The selective binding properties lower the risk of side effects and may be a useful alternative to conventional opioid and NSAIDs for chronic pain.

  • Outlook:  Clinical research underway in more than 11,000 patients indicates a meaningful value as compared to the placebo control group. Placebo groups are those people who receive a substance with no therapeutic effect. Study candidates are people who have experienced chronic low back pain for more than three months.  Comparative studies demonstrated better pain relief with Tanezumab then the narcotic analgesic oxycodone and the non-steroidal anti-inflammatory naproxen.
  • Therapy Administration: Tanezumab is a subcutaneous injectable product given once every eight weeks for 56 weeks of treatment for a total of seven injections.
  • Side Effect Profile:  Common side effects classified as temporary and minor were a headache, cold-like symptoms, tingling, itchiness, numbness, swelling, or hypersensitivity. Some patients experienced lower tendon reflexes.
  • Status:  Currently in Phase 3 Trials, the US Food and Drug Administration (FDA) fast tracked Tanezumab for the indication of chronic pain, including lower back pain, as of June 2017.

 NKTR-181 Mu-receptor is one of the several opioid receptors in the brain to alleviate pain. NKTR-181, by Nektar Therapeutics, is designed as a full mu-opioid agonist that shows positive results from the SUMMIT-07 Phase 3 study.  It is a new chemical substance, first in-class opioid analgesia without the euphoria that can lead to narcotic abuse.  

  • Outlook: Data from the study indicates substantial analgesic pain relief in patients with chronic low back pain and a notable reduction in abuse potential.
  • Therapy Administration:  Oral tablets administered twice a day. Patients are titrated to tolerated, effective dose range between 100mg to 400mg twice daily.
  • Side Effect Profile:  nausea, constipation
  • Status: The Food and Drug Administration (FDA) allowed NKTR-181 Fast Track designation for the treatment of chronic pain in moderate to severe significance. It completed a phase 3 study in 2017.

 

MPC-06-ID (rexlemestrocel-L) in development by Mesoblast Ltd, therapeutic approach is cell therapy, in particular, the undifferentiated stem cells, called mesenchymal precursor cells (MPCs). The indication is degenerative disc disease resulting in chronic low back pain in candidates who have exhausted conservative treatments with no pain relief.

  • Outlook: A randomized, placebo-controlled clinical trial demonstrated anti-inflammatory and pain reduction properties, with the potential to offer stability and strength to the spinal discs. Rexlemestrocel-L treatment was well-tolerated and improved function over 24 months compared with control therapies.
  • Therapy Administration:  Injected into the damaged disc as an outpatient clinic procedure.
  • Side Effect Profile:  Well-tolerated with minimal or no residual pain
  • Status: Ongoing Phase 3 clinical study recruitment by Mesoblast in 30 locations across the United States.

 

Ultrasound trigger liposomes use device design and medication targeting in a sono-sensitive drug delivery system. Boston Children’s’ hospital development of personalized, patient controlled, on-demand local anesthesia pain management device is non-invasive. The objective is to improve pain management and promote improved patient functioning with the replacement of addictive opioids.

  • Outlook: System utilizes ultrasound to release nerve blocking medication in targeted pain relief.  The patient adjusts and regulates the intensity, timing, and duration of ultrasound waves. Micron sized liposomes are artificial sacs filled with a nerve-blocking drug. The walls of the liposome are sensitive to ultrasonic waves and release medication on patient demand.
  • Therapy Administration:  Injection of liposomes is in a hospital setting directly into the affected tissue. A small, home use ultrasound device allows self-directed treatment. Liposomes have three-day duration after insertion.
  • Side Effect Profile:  Repeatable results obtained with no detectable toxicity or tissue reaction
  • Status:  Ongoing research trials. The active liposomal medication ingredients are already FDA approved with the delivery system is under development.
