Four Sleeping Positions for Dealing with Lower Back Pain

There are some ways to go to bed at night and find a position that helps your lower back pain and not feed it. We've come up with our names – but you can call them whatever you like!

The Jockey

If lying on your back hurts, only rest on your side and place a pillow on your knees and fall asleep. Ensure your shoulder connects with the mattress as well as that entire side of your body. Do not keep sleeping on the same side though as this might lead to muscle imbalance or scoliosis.

The Baby

Having a herniated disk can be helped by achieving a fetal position. Just lay on your back before rolling gently onto your side. Raise your knees toward your chest and then softly curl your torso to meet them. Again, switch sides every couple of nights to guard against muscle imbalance/scoliosis.

The Face Plant

In general, sleeping on your front isn't the best for lower back pain sufferers as it adds stress to the neck – however, there is a way to sleep in this position and conform it to your benefit. To do this, simply put a pillow beneath your lower abdomen/pelvis which removes pressure from the back. Based on how you feel, you can decide for yourself whether or not you wish to use a pillow for your face.

The Netflix & Chill 

If you prefer a recliner to a bed, this position is especially helpful to someone suffering from isthmic spondylolisthesis by creating an angle between your thighs and torso and lessening pressure on the spine. 

Whichever position you choose, it's all about alignment so wherever you notice a gap between yourself and the mattress when aligning your ears/shoulders/hips, use a pillow to fill them in. Also, be careful when turning around.
