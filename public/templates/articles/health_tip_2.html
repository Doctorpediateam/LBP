Prevention is Better Than Intervention

However old fashioned it may seem, the proverb attributed to Benjamin Franklin, “an ounce of prevention is worth a pound of cure” is an appropriate piece of advice for preventing lower back pain.

There are many outside and inside factors that influence lower back pain such as previous injuries, obesity, genetics, conditioning, activity level, and others. By taking a few measures ahead of time, you can minimize a lot of pain, disability, and time away from the things you love to do!

Prehabilitation is a concept built on the foundational common sense notion that “prevention is better than intervention.” Doing things  proactively to prevent or limit damage, disease, sickness, and the enduring consequences are part of the best practices culture.

We have a hands-on opportunity to create practical, active, and preemptive approaches by taking the initiative. It is a mix of combining improved posture, muscle tensions, and shoring up weakened tissues.  

Safety is number one! Protection and the right gear will reduce the chances of sustaining an injury and provides added support and reinforcement to existing lower back pain. Check out back braces and lumbar support belts.  These products look after the delicate lumbosacral and sacroiliac region of the lower spinal cord providing support and spine protection.  They are a valuable tool available in lightweight, enhanced support, adjustable elastic, made from breathable fabrics and neoprene. This is not your grandmother’s corset!

To sustain motivation, connect and network with like-minded people. Create your tribe to engage in a mild conditioning and exercise routines. Exercise and stretching are healthy!

Activities and movement develop the muscles and can significantly reduce pain and keep conditions from getting worse or happening at all. As your muscle and tissue strength grows, you may enjoy moving to the next level of physical workouts or sports. For now, just walk!

Swimming is the best low-impact activity as it is less strenuous on the muscles and joints and allows for greater mobility as you use multiple muscle groups. The water is a buffered protectant in its buoyancy. The main benefit is swimming’s ability to relieve stress on the joints while at the same time strengthening the muscles. It is a two-for-one activity!  

Not sure you can swim? Take a few lessons; it is an excellent way to heal your back and meet people on the same wave length!

There are many books on back exercises that are available. As your lower back pain resolves and the muscular support system strengthens, there is so much more you will be able to do. Are you are ready for the next challenge? Always use suitable equipment and weights for your body size and condition. If you start out with anything that is too much or too heavy will leave you discouraged and may worsen the condition. Never give up!

Any number of fun activities can help strengthen your back, be it free weights, resistance bands, foam rollers, exercise balls, elliptical trainers, platform step, Nordic trekking poles, stair climber, or treadmill. But, always speak to your doctor first. It’s about safety, health, and persistence!
