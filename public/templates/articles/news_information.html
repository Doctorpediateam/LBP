Popular Low Back Pain Neuromodulator Gabapentin Not Effective

Results reviewed from eight controlled studies do not support the use of gabapentin (Neurontin) in the treatment of chronic low back pain. This therapeutic classification also included the medication pregablin (Lyrica). In a benefit versus risk analysis, the use of these drugs linked to a substantial possibility of side effects and adverse reactions. The research failed to demonstrate pain relief.

Of note, patients taking pregablin did worse on the drug than taking other chronic low back pain analgesics. Side effects included confusion, tiredness, lightheadedness, and visual changes.

If you are taking this drug, do not stop this abruptly. Contact your doctor for further information.

Lower Back Pain’s Relationship with Estrogen

A study implies that lumbar spine disc degeneration and lower back pain may benefit from estrogen replacement therapy. One cause of estrogen deficiency is as a result of declining hormone levels in menopause. Magnetic resonance imaging (MRI) of more than 1,500 patients showed significant changes in disc degeneration within the first 15 years after the onset of menopause. The outcome was a correlation that estrogen replacement therapy may be useful in prevention or treatment of chronic low back pain. More research in a larger, expanded population is required.

Also, the side effect profile of estrogen replacement therapy is to be considered. The benefits must outweigh the risks are estrogen side effects are numerous, including cardiovascular effects, dementia, cancer, and stroke.

 

Low Back Pain Treatment: Sugar Pills are Effective

A placebo or sugar pill contains inert ingredients that do not exert any biological effect. In the treatment of low back pain, patients received a non-steroidal anti-inflammatory, naproxen (Naprosyn), or a muscle relaxant, diazepam (Valium), or a placebo. The research was a randomized, double-blind clinical trial. There was no difference in relief of low back pain between the three treatment modalities. There is expanding evidence that medications do not improve lower back pain. Follow-up with patients, at one week and three months, demonstrated improvement regardless of the treatment option.

This data point is an important finding as there are risks associated with certain drugs, such as addiction, gastrointestinal bleeding, sedation, and overmedicating.

 

What a Shock! Low Back Pain Not Relieved with Electric Current

There was no significant improvement in chronic low back pain with the administration of radio-frequency denervation treatment. During the three randomized trials, the participants were not blinded, meaning each was aware of the treatment they received.  Also, they received a routine exercise plan and emotional support therapy. This radio-frequency denervation procedure is a conventional treatment; however, credible proof as to the effectiveness is lacking.

 

Low Back Pain Responsive to Yoga

Yoga stretches and movements appear to help improve function and lessen low back pain in comparison to no exercise or movement at all. A synopsis of multiple studies in which all individuals had chronic low back pain found the invention helped relieve the symptoms in the majority of patients. About 5% of patients did experience more discomfort. In deciding a treatment option, the health benefits must outweigh any risks in an informed choice.

 

Mind Body Connection: Low Back Pain

Stress reduction techniques, cognitive behavioral therapy, and mindfulness appear to influence pain levels in patients with low back pain. Outcomes indicated improvement and expanded physical mobility with less pain. The study ran for 26 weeks of treatment. Investigators observed that those participants who received mindfulness-based stress reduction (MBSR) or cognitive behavior therapy (CBT) experienced more meaningful improvement over those treated with usual care. MBSR teaches moment-to-moment acceptance and awareness. CBT trains to alter behaviors and thoughts. These outcomes point to an active therapeutic alternative.

 

Self-Care: Low Back Pain Smartphone Apps Content Quality

Evaluated for content quality and recommendations for evidence-based interventions, the number of self-management low back pain apps found was 723 of which 61 were part of the review study. Apps aggregate score comprised a level of engagement, features, the value of information supplied, reputation, and trustworthiness. The data generated concluded that while many endorse therapeutic options, a small number is a superior quality. The primary recommendations given by the apps included exercise without a healthcare professional’s approval, booklets to read, and further online education to view. These apps are only one tool in the management of low back pain. Always be safe – consult your physician before starting on any treatment plan.

 

Low Back Pain: Anti-inflammatory Diet

Inflammation plays a role in injury and pain. Non-steroidal anti-inflammatory medications are the standard of treatment for pain relief. Studies have looked at anti-inflammatory nutrition and diets as a possible adjunct to care. These diets – the Mediterranean diet is one example – are high in omega-3 fatty acids, the standard leafy green vegetables and assorted fruits, fiber and whole grains, as well as calcium-rich foods. As with any change, gastrointestinal symptoms may occur, such as diarrhea, bloating, constipation, and nausea. Before making any therapeutic changes in the self-treatment of lower back pain, first discuss all options with a doctor and dietitian.
